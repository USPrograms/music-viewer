She is a number girl, counting out the world to a static sum,

VERSE 1

Making it succumb to a fixed amount, something she can count on:

 

The constant truth of numeric proof.

 

 

 

She counts to understand: fingers on her hand, pentatonic scale,

 

Lines upon a stave, letting five prevail, saving her from four’s

 

Common time and notes quartered up and drawn.

 

 

 

__Infinite arrays counting out her days, numbers everywhere

 

A sequential prayer, counting to contrive: fibonacci five.

 

 

 

__Four is not enough when the road is rough, when life calls her bluff;

 

She must count to five, infinite arrays counting out her days.

 

 

 

She is arithmomanic. She’s in a panic unless it all

VERSE 2

Can be made to count to the right amount, to the perfect digit:

 

Her fingers fidget, repeating five.

 

 

 

She knows she pays the price, makes the sacrifice, in exact amounts,

 

Keeping her accounts: nickels are a smile, five of them a grin,

 

Making twenty-five, momentary win.

 

 

 

__Turning to a loss: twenty-five a square, two bits double cross.

 

Bittersweet affair, her beloved five, flirting with a four.

 

 

 

__Searching for one more, counting breaths indrawn, finding five restores

 

For a single span her numeric plan, and she can count on.

VERSE 3

She counts to make some sense of this life immense: fingers on her hand

 

Try to understand, try to keep it small, as she counts it all,

 

Fives she must construe, counting chaos true.

 

 

 

__Infinites accrue, what else can she do? As she thinks it through,

 

Blue notes make her blue, pentatonic nixed, something to be fixed.

 

 

 

__What else can she do? Infinites accrue, add another four

 

Notes, a rest at ten, add another five, then begin again….
